Australian Catering Industry Insights Dashboard

A comprehensive view of Australia’s restaurant and catering ecosystem — from city distribution and cuisine growth to cost structure and supply networks.

Restaurant Density by City

A comparison of restaurant concentration across Australian cities — showing where the country’s culinary diversity and business opportunities thrive.

Sydney and Melbourne lead with the highest restaurant counts per capita, while Brisbane and Perth display strong growth trends linked to population expansion and tourism recovery.

Cuisine Revenue Trends (2000–2025)

Longitudinal revenue data revealing how Australians’ culinary preferences have evolved — from traditional European cuisines to Asian fusion and plant-based trends.

Fusion cuisines have seen exponential growth since 2015, while conventional Western categories plateaued. The pandemic temporarily reduced revenue but sparked innovation in takeaway models.

Overall Catering Revenue Outlook

This visual aggregates national catering revenue performance to highlight macro-economic health, recovery patterns, and long-term business sustainability.

Seasonal spikes align with holiday and event seasons. Revenue stabilisation post-2022 indicates renewed consumer confidence and operational scalability across small to medium enterprises.

National Catering Market Size Growth

Illustrates the total market value of Australia’s catering sector over time, capturing shifts in consumer demand, event culture, and service innovation.

A steady upward trajectory reflects resilience despite global disruptions — with major rebounds following COVID-19 lockdowns and continued growth projected through 2025.

Restaurant Cost Allocation

This visualisation breaks down how an average Australian restaurant allocates its expenses — offering insights into which operational factors impact profitability the most.

Labour and ingredient costs dominate total expenditure, while rent and utilities remain major fixed expenses. Marketing and logistics contribute smaller yet strategic portions of the budget.

Australia’s Food Supply Chain

Mapping the origins of catering ingredients across Australia and abroad — revealing the country’s dependence on both local producers and international trade routes.

Local sourcing dominates fresh produce and dairy, whereas imported ingredients like spices and seafood are primarily routed from Asia and Europe, highlighting the industry’s global interconnection.
